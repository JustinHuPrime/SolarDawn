Solar Dawn Guide

Solar Dawn is a 4X digital board game featuring simultaneous turn resolution as you fly modular spacecraft around a hex-grid board.

You play as one of up to six nations racing to industrialize space. Your goal is to become the dominant power in space by controlling the majority of the industrial power in the system, whether through peaceful growth or violent destruction.

The Board

The game board is a hex grid, where the distance from the center of one hex to the center of any other hex represents 0.1 AU. The exact layout of the board can vary by scenario. For the default campaign board, it is a stylized recreation of the solar system (as in, only vaguely accurate positioning of celestial bodies).

The standard campaign board consists of the sun, eight planets, seventeen moons, and many asteroids and kuiper belt objects, with more ore-bearing bodies closer to the sun and more water-bearing bodies further from the sun.

  • Mercury and Venus can be landed on to mine for ore
  • The Earth can't be landed on at all, but you can only build habitats while in orbit of Earth
  • The Moon can be landed on to mine for water and ore
  • Mars can be landed on to mine for water and ore
  • Phobos and Deimos can be rendezvoused with to mine for ore
  • The asteroid belt can be rendezvoused with to mine for ore, and sometimes for water
  • The four gas giants, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune, can have fuel skimmed from them while in orbit
  • The fourteen moons of the gas giants can be landed on to mine for water and often also for ore
  • The kuiper belt can be rendezvoused with to mine for water and sometimes for ore

Sequence of Play

Each round is split into three phases - logistics, combat, and movement. During each phase, you and your opponents will give orders to your spacecraft stacks which will be simultaneously resolved, before looping back around to the logistics phase to start the next turn.

During the logistics phase you can move modules between rendezvoused stacks, make new stacks, move resources within and between rendezvoused stacks, mine and refine resources, repair, salvage, and build modules, and attempt to board undefended modules.

During the combat phase, you can shoot your guns at targets and arm warheads.

During the movement phase, you can conduct engine burns, orbital rendezvouses, landings, and take-offs. At the end of this phase, objects move according to their velocity and update their velocities based on the gravity effects of nearby bodies.

Modules and Stacks

Your units are rocket stacks, each of which consists of some number of modules in no particular structure. Stacks move together, share resources, and take damage as a group. Stacks can only be split or combined during the logistics phase.

Combat

There are three means of damaging enemy spacecraft:

  1. You can shoot at them with guns, although this is incredibly ineffective at ranges greater than a single hex. A single gun deals one point of damage on a successful hit, but the hit rate goes down by half (from 100%) per hex of range.
  2. You can hit them with a warhead, either in a stack of its own or in a stack with other modules, like a fuel tank and an engine. Warheads deal damage equal to one third (rounded up) of the number of modules on each ship hit, and can hit multiple ships at once. Warheads are the primary means of dealing damage to enemy ships, but can be dodged or shot down, depending on how you construct your missile stack. Warheads have a damage radius of just over half a hex, and will detonate the instant an enemy stack is within range.
  3. You can manoeuver them into a planet. This instantly destroys the stack and leaves behind no debris. This is, however, exceedingly difficult to achieve against stacks with even minimal engine thrust.

When taking damage, first: intact armour plates are damaged into damaged armour plates per point of damage until no intact plates remain or all damage has been assigned. Second: non-destroyed modules are randomly selected to take damage per point of damage remaining, converting intact modules into damaged modules and damaged modules into destroyed modules.

Damaged modules can be repaired into intact modules at the cost of ⅒ of the module's mass by a habitat or a factory during the logistics phase. Destroyed modules, however, can only be scrapped for materials equivalent to ½ of the module's mass by a factory during the logistics phase. Note that a destroyed or damaged module still masses the same as an intact module.

The Economy

There are two main resource cycles in the game. First, your stacks mine ore, which is refined into materials, which is turned into modules to upgrade stacks. Second, your stacks mine water which is refined into fuel (or you skim fuel directory) which is used to move your stacks around.

Strategy

Your goal in the default scenario is to control the majority of the victory points on the board. You can achieve this both by building more things that grant victory point as well by destroying or capturing things that grant victory points from your opponents.

Ship Design
